The number of data protection and cybersecurity laws that exist across the globe continues to grow rapidly. These laws are often inconsistent and sometimes even conflict with one another, increasing regulatory burdens on covered entities. Certain laws are extraterritorial and apply not just to entities that are located in a particular jurisdiction. Some jurisdictions have multiple laws that cut across different industries, resulting in entities confronting layers of compliance obligations. This portal is part of an ongoing project to present the details of the most active laws and to synthesize key privacy questions across multiple statutes.

The tool currently captures information across an array of data protection and cybersecurity laws and regulations in the United States, and will evolve to include key international laws and regulations. This information can help you understand existing legal obligations of entities in a particular jurisdiction and the rights of residents or individuals residing in that jurisdiction. If you would like more information on a specific law that has not yet been analyzed, please contact us.
